**PART A**

1. **Title:** The Endogeneity of Miscalibration: Impossibility and Escape in Scored Reporting
2. **Source:** arXiv (2605.07671) — Lovén and Tarkoma
3. **Publication date:** May 8, 2026
4. **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07671
5. **Jurisdiction / institution:** Academic preprint
6. **One-sentence summary:** Proves a formal impossibility: any oversight mechanism using a strictly proper scoring rule to elicit truthful AI reports endogenously generates the conditions for its own calibration failure, because the optimal approval function the principal must use to screen types necessarily incentivizes misreporting under combined objectives.
7. **Why selected:** Third formal impossibility result now in play alongside SLT and AIT; applies specifically to scored oversight mechanisms—a category that includes most current AI evaluation and assurance practices. `[PROXIMITY SIGNAL — RJB PUBLICATION: P1, P2]` — "optimal oversight is self-undermining" = a formal instantiation of the Audit Illusion at the mechanism-design level; "this is not a design error that a cleverer principal could avoid; it is a structural impossibility" is independent confirmation of the ontological-not-epistemic failure distinction; escape via step-function threshold ≅ ABCF's REFUSE/HALT gate under strict CNI.
8. **Relevant ABCF components:** Audit Illusion; structural causal identifiability; admissibility checks; execution gates; Strict CNI (REFUSE/HALT for Ψ-state events)
9. **Relevance level:** Direct relevance

**PART B**

- **ABCF coherence:** High
- **ABCF alignment:** Strong
- **Infringement or tension:** None
- **Structural analysis:** This is the most formally significant new item in Run 2. The endogeneity result proves that scored oversight (the principal rates the agent's confidence report and uses the score to determine approval for autonomous action) cannot be calibrated because the approval function needed for screening types also corrupts reporting incentives. The perturbation is not avoidable through cleverer design; it is structural. This is an independent proof of the Audit Illusion at the mechanism-design layer, derived from scoring theory rather than causal geometry—and it reaches the same conclusion: scored evaluation cannot substitute for structural intervention. The constructive escape (step-function approval threshold) maps closely to ABCF's binary REFUSE/HALT gate: the only way to avoid the endogeneity is to make the approval function non-graded, i.e., a hard gate.
- **Known facts:** Impossibility holds for all strictly proper scoring rules; closed-form perturbation formula derived; step-function escape achieves first-best screening; submitted May 8, 2026.
- **Inferences:** This result, if it gains traction, closes a gap in the governance literature: it formally defeats the assumption that better evaluation tools can substitute for structural governance intervention. That is a formal argument for the structural necessity of ABCF-class gates, from mechanism design rather than causal identifiability.
- **Unknowns:** Whether this result will connect to AI governance discourse or remain in mechanism-design literature; peer review status.
- **ABCF classification:** Coherent and aligned
