## Section 7 — Research positioning summary

The corpus, across four test-session runs over two days, shows a field building the **outer shell** of AI governance — transparency codes, provider documentation, impact assessments, audits, procurement standards, agent-delegation protocols, monitoring profiles, and liability rulings — while leaving the **structural substrate** ABCF-v1 addresses unspecified. The recurring result is that visibility, documentation, certification, credentialing, and predictive competence each stop one layer short of authorization, causal attribution, admissibility, and substantive authority.

**1. Where the field is moving relative to ABCF.** Toward operational governance and toward the *diagnosis* of structural attribution failure — but not toward the *characterization* of that failure as an ontological rank-deficiency limit. The strongest movement is in the structural-attribution cluster, which has now crossed formal, legal, judicial, empirical, and policy registers independently. The fastest movement is the execution-gate cluster at the protocol layer (IETF), which is operationalizing.

**2. Concepts most at risk of independent convergence (1–3 months).** The execution-gate / admissibility architecture (P1/P2/P3) — protocol groups are converging on authorization-before-execution and may standardize a gate without the identifiability condition, partially absorbing ABCF's surface claim. Second, the epistemic-authority cluster (P1/P2), accumulating fastest behaviorally. *Convergence risk is a research-positioning observation here, not an IP/prior-art finding — that is Layer 2.*

**3. Concepts structurally ahead with no visible convergence.** The ontological framing of structural indeterminacy (SI as a property of the triple C,A,τ); the substantive-vs-nominal authority distinction with fail-closed gating; and the entire P4/P7/P8 set (economic surplus, recognition neutrality, civic legibility), which the field is not approaching at all.

**4. Highest-priority research actions visible from the corpus.**
   (a) Draft the positioning piece that fixes the field-vs-ABCF boundary: "existing regimes build recognition/documentation/certification/delegation artifacts; ABCF defines when those become structurally sufficient for authorization and admissibility." The execution-gate cluster is the natural attachment point.
   (b) Sharpen the P3 technical position against the agent-authority and world-model vectors *before* the IETF gate convergence matures.
   (c) Open dedicated P4/P7/P8 search vectors — the zero-coverage is a monitoring blind spot, not a field absence claim.
